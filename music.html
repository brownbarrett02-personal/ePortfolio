My Music Journey

Music has been a constant thread running through my life long before engineering became my focus. I started playing [instrument] at age [X], drawn in by [what attracted you — a teacher, a piece of music, a family influence, etc.].

Through my years with the Youth Orchestras of San Antonio, I had the opportunity to [describe what that experience meant to you — performances, growth, community, etc.]. Playing in an ensemble taught me things I couldn't learn anywhere else: how to listen as carefully as you play, how to subordinate individual ego to a collective sound, and how patience and precision compound over time.

When I arrived at Texas A&M, continuing to play was never a question. Joining the Chamber Orchestra has been one of the most grounding parts of my college experience — a weekly reminder that engineering isn't the only discipline that rewards rigor and craft. There's something about sight-reading a difficult passage and watching it come together over weeks of rehearsal that maps almost perfectly onto the iterative problem-solving I do in the lab.
